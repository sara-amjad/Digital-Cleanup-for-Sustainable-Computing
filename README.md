# ♻️ Sustainable Computing through Digital Cleanup: Addressing E-Waste in the Digital Age  
**Author:** Sara Amjad | **Date:** May 2026  

> **Academic Context:**  
This repository contains an academic research project developed for Unit 41: Digital Sustainability under the Pearson BTEC HND in Digital Technologies (Cyber Security). The research explores sustainable computing practices through digital cleanup strategies, focusing on e-waste reduction, energy-efficient data management, cloud optimisation, and environmentally responsible digital behaviour.

## 📌 Overview
This repository presents a research study examining how digital cleanup practices contribute to sustainable computing and environmental responsibility within modern digital infrastructures.
The research explores the relationship between digital waste, cloud storage growth, energy consumption, and carbon emissions generated by data centres. It evaluates how practices such as deleting redundant files, optimising cloud storage, extending hardware lifecycles, and implementing green computing strategies can reduce environmental impact.
The study combines technical, behavioural, and policy-based approaches to support environmentally sustainable digital ecosystems and responsible computing practices.

- ♻️ Digital cleanup and sustainable computing

## 🧠 Key Research Areas

### 🔹 Digital Waste and Sustainability
The research examines how redundant files, unused cloud storage, and excessive digital data contribute to increased energy usage and environmental impact.

### 🔹 Green Computing Practices
The study analyses sustainable IT approaches including cloud optimisation, hardware reuse, energy-efficient infrastructure, and responsible computing strategies.

### 🔹 Environmental Impact of Data Storage
The paper evaluates how data centres consume energy continuously to maintain digital storage systems, contributing to global carbon emissions.

### 🔹 Behavioural and Policy-Based Sustainability
The research explores how user awareness, organisational policies, and sustainability frameworks support long-term digital environmental responsibility.

## 📊 Research Analysis and Findings

The study identified a strong relationship between digital storage growth and energy consumption within digital infrastructures.

Key findings include:

- ⚡ Increased cloud storage contributes to higher energy usage  
- ☁️ Data centre operations generate measurable carbon emissions  
- ♻️ Digital cleanup practices can reduce unnecessary storage loads  
- 🌱 Green computing strategies support sustainable digital ecosystems  
- 🏢 Organisational sustainability policies improve digital responsibility  
- 📉 Cloud optimisation and efficient data management reduce environmental impact  


## 🚀 Recommended Sustainable Computing Strategies

- 🗑️ Regular digital cleanup and redundant data removal  
- ☁️ Cloud storage optimisation and deduplication  
- 🔋 Adoption of renewable-powered data centres  
- 💻 Hardware reuse and lifecycle extension  
- 📚 Sustainability awareness and behavioural change initiatives  
- 📊 Carbon monitoring and energy-efficient infrastructure planning
